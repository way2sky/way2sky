About




I earned an MFA in Interactive Media from USC's School of Cinematic Arts. I'm also a recipient of the USC Annenberg Graduate Fellowship in Cinematic Arts. My thesis research focused on creating and applying interactive applications, games, and technologies to improve emotional health. My research interests also include data visualization, human computer interaction, and cognitive science. My bachelors degree was in Interdisciplinary Computing in the Arts from the University of California at San Diego.

I have presented at Dorkbot, MeaningfulPlay, ESCoNS, and Games for Health among others.
