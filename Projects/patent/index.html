Customizable Watch (2005)

A patent for a modular reconfigurable wrist watch design. Removable band pieces allow various permutations of the device, giving the user freedom to remix features and there locations relative to the wrist for different occasions and needs. Full patent pdf.

A similar concept involving modular UI design and tangible lego-like electronic pieces is David Merrill's "Siftables" developed at the MIT Media Lab.

Like the customizable watch, this interaction platform emphasizes physical interaction. Siftables involves moving and rearranging multiple small devices into groupings to perform a wide array of digital media and information processing tasks.

Some history. The plenty of time I had during junior high school allowed me to make best friends with the likes of Popular Science and PC Magazine. Later, in 2003, I sketched up the initial design of the watch. Desperately wanting to see this worn on people's wrists, I thought it would be cool and I sent the patent to Steve Jobs. I never thought about money stuff at that time. But in reply to my enthusiasm and youthful naivete, Apple's legal department mailed a letter to me explaining that they don't take unsolicited ideas. Oops.

Improved usability through tangible objects rather than navigation through complicated menu structures.
